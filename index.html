Research Line

My research in Zhejiang University mainly focuses on the design of location-based services for indoor spaces. We are exploring the available localization technologies that can be adapted to heterogeneous indoor environments and also offer the location-aware services to a wide range of devices. We have been working on the analysis of sensors available on smartphones, such as the Wi-Fi/Bluetooth chipsets, camera, accelerometer, gyroscope, and digital compass. We have also been studying the wireless network infrastructures based on Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, or RFID. Based on the localization technologies that obtain the positions and other contextual information of the smartphones, we have also been developing the ideas of analyzing the smartphone user behaviors for the needs of location-based marketing/advertising, environmental quality improvement, and congestion/security management. I am advised by Prof.Gang Chen and co-advised by Prof.Lidan Shou.

I am also interested in spatio-temporal data management techniques, especially those for the mobility data obtained from indoor space under different indoor localization technologies. In the year 2014, I was a visiting researcher (advised by Associate Prof. Hua Lu) in the Center for Data-intensive Systems (Daisy), Aalborg University. My research in Daisy focuses on the novel data cleansing, query processing, and data analysis approaches in complex indoor environments.
